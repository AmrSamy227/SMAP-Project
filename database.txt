


What did the database team build?

designed a full relational database architecture covering most core platform features, including:
Users and authentication (users)
Roles and permissions (roles)
User profiles and verification
Listings/properties
Images and amenities
Bookings/reservations
Reviews and ratings
Messaging and notifications
Availability and pricing


How ?
followed structured relational database design principles.

	1. Centralized User Model
	2. Proper Normalization
	3.Strong Use of Foreign Keys
	4. Modular Design


Why did they build it this way?
	1. Scalability
	2. Maintainability
	3. Analytics & Reporting
	4. Security
or 

Why the Database Was Designed This Way
	To support future scalability.
	To keep the system organized and easy to maintain.
	To separate different business functionalities clearly.
	To model real-world booking workflows accurately.
	To allow future feature expansion without redesigning the system.


Project Success Evaluation
	The project was successful in designing a strong core database architecture.
	The system demonstrates good understanding of backend and relational database concepts.


Strengths of the Design
	Well-structured and organized schema.
	Clear relationships between entities.
	Scalable architecture for future development.
	Good separation of concerns between modules.
	Reflects real-world business processes effectively.
	Easy to extend with additional features later.

Weaknesses / Limitations
	No integrated payment system.
	No audit log or activity tracking system.
	Large number of relationships may affect performance without optimization.
	Some complexity may be unnecessary for an MVP version.
